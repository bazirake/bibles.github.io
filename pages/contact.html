About Scripture Union Rwanda

Vision

Scripture Union Rwanda operates in Rwanda since 1969 and is a part of international family of scripture union movements that began in 1867 and now operate in over 130 countries across the world. We are a Christian organisation working in partnership with churches. More than 1,000 volunteers are involved in helping to run our activities in different areas of the country.

We focus on:

  • Our Vision: Children and Youth following jesus filled with hope and transforming the nation of families of Rwanda.
  • Our Mission: Nurturing children and Youth, strengthening family life and helping people engange with God through

    the Bible, that they may follow Jesus Christ and be instsrumental in transfoorming their communities.

  • SU aims :

    A: To make God's good news known to children, young people and families and

    B. To encourage people of all pages to meet God daily through the Bible and prayer so that they may come to personal faith in our Lord Jesus Christ, Grow in Christian maturity and become both committed church members and servants of a world in need;

Children & Youth Ministry
Activities of SU in Kigali

Achievements

Scipture Union activities include programs for systematic Bible reading, children's missions, family ministries, residential holiday activities and camps, work in schools, training activities, youth work, the publishing and distribution of Christian literature and audio-visuals but there are special programs also like street children project.

Bible Ministries and Bible Guides/Cards

This ministry writes the Bible reading notes for each year, and it generally promotes reading of the Holy Bible. This department is also engaged in evangelistic activities, such as organising crusades. It motivates local committees established in 42 localities of the country, gathering Christians from different denominations.

School Ministries and Camps

Recently Scripture Union Rwanda launched a new program assisting street children. Thanks to this new program 30 children have been brought back to normal life. They now go back to school and they are given lunch and taught the word of God every day in the SU compound.

Holiday Activities

The target group is especially the youth in secondary schools and universities and it is reached through a series of organised visits and seminars.
